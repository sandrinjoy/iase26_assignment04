## Exercise 2: grammar-based fuzzer

The grammar-based fuzzer reaches **Exit Code 134 (`SIGABRT`)**.

- **Reason for crash:** The fuzzer generates an array nested to a depth of 300 (e.g., `[[[[...]]]]`). This deep nesting causes a stack overflow due to recursion limits in the target program's parser during syntax analysis, causing it to abort.
- **Why mutational/lexical fuzzers cannot reach it:**
  - **Lexical Fuzzer:** Generates characters randomly without grammatical rules. The probability of randomly producing 300 matching pairs of brackets (with syntactic validity) is extremely close to zero.
  - **Mutational Fuzzer:** Applies small, local edits to a seed input. It cannot systematically grow the nesting structure to depth 300 while keeping the intermediate mutated inputs syntactically valid (which is required by the parser to recurse deeply). Any invalid bracket sequence is rejected early before deep recursion is triggered. In contrast, the grammar-based fuzzer uses the EBNF rules to systematically derive deeply nested but syntactically correct nested structures.
